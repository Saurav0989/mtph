Block on a Frictionless Incline

physicsmechanics/dynamicsdifficulty 2/5#newton#incline#free-body

A block of mass $m$ rests on a frictionless incline that makes an angle $\theta$ with the
horizontal. Find the magnitude of its acceleration down the slope.

Answer & solution

Answer

$ a = g\sin\theta $

Solution

Perpendicular to the incline the block is in equilibrium; along the surface the only driving
force is the down-slope component of the weight, so Newton's second law gives
$$a = \frac{m g \sin\theta}{m} = g\sin\theta.$$
